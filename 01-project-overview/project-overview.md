# Project Overview

## Lab Title
Third-Party Vendor Risk Assessment — CloudSync Solutions

## Purpose

This lab simulates a formal third-party risk assessment conducted by a GRC analyst at **Meridian Financial Group**, a fictional mid-sized financial services company. The assessment evaluates **CloudSync Solutions**, a SaaS HR and workforce management platform being considered for internal use.

The goal is to determine whether CloudSync meets Meridian's security, privacy, and compliance requirements before granting the vendor access to sensitive employee and operational data.

---

## Scope

### In Scope
- Security posture of CloudSync Solutions as a data processor
- Controls protecting employee PII and payroll data
- Vendor's cloud infrastructure and data handling practices (AWS, multi-tenant)
- Compliance certifications: SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001
- Access control, encryption, incident response, and business continuity practices

### Out of Scope
- Physical security of CloudSync office locations
- Assessment of CloudSync's own vendors (fourth-party risk)
- Source code review or penetration testing
- Real-time monitoring of CloudSync's environment

---

## Methodology

This assessment follows a structured TPRM lifecycle:

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| 1. Vendor Intake | Collect vendor profile, data flows, and initial risk classification |
| 2. Inherent Risk Scoring | Score the vendor's risk before considering controls |
| 3. Security Questionnaire | Send detailed questionnaire to vendor and evaluate responses |
| 4. Control Mapping | Map vendor controls to NIST CSF and ISO 27001 |
| 5. Risk Analysis | Identify gaps, score residual risk, build risk register |
| 6. Remediation Planning | Develop POA&M for identified gaps |
| 7. Decision & Reporting | Produce executive summary and risk-based recommendation |

---

## Risk Rating Scale

| Rating | Score Range | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | 9–10 | Immediate action required; vendor approval blocked |
| High | 7–8 | Significant gaps; conditional approval with remediation |
| Medium | 4–6 | Notable gaps; acceptance with monitoring |
| Low | 1–3 | Minor gaps; standard approval with review cycle |
